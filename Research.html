Research Focus

Compressed hydrogen production technologies with process intensification

Over 10 million tons of hydrogen are produced in the US annually. Most of this hydrogen comes from steam-methane reforming, which requires many steps such as reforming, separation, and compression. Process intensification combines unit operations in a beneficial manner to improve overall efficiency. Processes such as CAPER provide produce compressed hydrogen at low voltage, low temperature, and with high efficiency.
